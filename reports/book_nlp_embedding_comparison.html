A vs B: LSA 384d scores consistently worse than LSA 100d on all cluster quality metrics (silhouette 0.014 vs 0.037, Davies-Bouldin 6.18 vs 4.22). More dimensions from the same TF-IDF vocabulary introduce noise rather than signal — the vocabulary is the ceiling, not the dimensionality. Adjusted Rand Index between A and B clusters: 0.4181 (moderate agreement — they broadly agree on groupings but differ on ~48% of nearest neighbours).
Low silhouette scores overall (0.039–0.012) are expected, not a failure. The cybernetics corpus spans pure mathematics, ecology, management science, philosophy, and fiction — no clean cluster boundaries exist. This score range is typical for highly interdisciplinary academic corpora.

6 · Key Findings & Recommendations

📌 What the results show

  • Method A wins on cluster tightness (silhouette 0.0388) — Method B scores lowest (0.0122)
  • Method C wins on cluster separation (Davies-Bouldin 3.68) — lower = better separated clusters
  • All silhouette scores are low (<0.05) — expected for an interdisciplinary corpus with no hard topic boundaries
  • A and B agree on ~52% of nearest neighbours (ARI=0.418) — A finds same-LDA-topic neighbours 57% of the time vs B at 52%
  • A and C agree on ~6% of nearest neighbours (ARI=0.146) — A finds same-LDA-topic neighbours 58% of the time vs C at 40%
  • B and C agree on ~7% of nearest neighbours (ARI=0.122) — B finds same-LDA-topic neighbours 56% of the time vs C at 40%
  • 6 books with empty summaries get sim=0.000 in LSA methods — a data quality issue, not a method failure
